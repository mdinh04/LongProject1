Resources Beyond the Classroom

This page helps Computer Science students discover useful opportunities outside of class, build experience, and connect with the CS community.

Internships

Internships help students gain real-world experience in software development, cybersecurity, data science, IT support, and other technical fields.

  • Search for internships through Handshake
  • Attend university career fairs
  • Apply on company websites
  • Build experience before graduation

Career Opportunities

Computer Science students can explore different career paths and prepare for jobs through resume workshops, interview practice, and professional guidance.

  • Resume and cover letter support
  • Interview preparation
  • Career fairs and employer events
  • Networking with professionals

CS Clubs

Student clubs help you meet other students, work on projects, attend events, and build your technical and leadership skills.

  • Join coding and technology clubs
  • Participate in hackathons
  • Collaborate on group projects
  • Connect with peers in CS

Research Opportunities

Students can work with faculty on research in areas such as artificial intelligence, machine learning, cybersecurity, and data science.

  • Work with faculty mentors
  • Join research labs and projects
  • Gain hands-on academic experience
  • Prepare for graduate study or industry roles

Why These Resources Matter

Student resources are important because they help Computer Science students grow outside of the classroom. Internships provide practical experience, clubs create a sense of community, career support helps students prepare for the future, and research opportunities allow students to explore advanced topics with faculty.

Together, these resources make the CS website more helpful, informative, and useful for both current and future students.
